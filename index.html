DEFENDER

The Land Rover Defender is one of the most iconic vehicles of all time. Over 2 million were built between 1947 and 2016. Due to its use on numerous expeditions across the world it was the first vehicle 60% of people in the developing world ever saw. The Land Rover is still used across the world for its rugged off-road capabilities but has also become somewhat of a collectors item with some examples costing upwards of Â£100 000.

I owned a Defender a few years ago, they are not a luxurious vehicle, they are not fast and they are definitely not economical, but they do provide a sense of adventure with every journey (as long as it starts that is).


A brief history of the Defender

  • 1948 The original Land Rover (Series I) is launched.
  • 1949 The British Army orders it first batch of Land Rovers
  • 1953 The Land Rover enters service with the Royal family
  • 1954 Long wheelbase model introduced (107in)
  • 1958 The Series II is launched.
  • 1966 The 500 000th Land Rover vehicle is produced.
  • 1971 The Land Rover Series III is launched in October. Also the year of the 750 000th Land Rover is produced
  • 1976 The 1 000 000th Land Rover is built.
  • 1990 The biggest change to the Land Rover came in late 1990, when it became the Land Rover Defender
  • 1994 The Land Rover Wolf was introduced the British Military and many are still in service to this day.
  • 1990 - 2016 During this period visually the Defender changed very little. There were a few upgrades to the engine and internal trim as well as a few special editions.
  • 2016 After a continuous run of 67 years production finally ended on 29 January 2016
  • 2019 The all new Land Rover Defender was launched
